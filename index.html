About

I'm a Ph.D. student at EdLab @ KAIST AI, advised by Prof. Edward Choi. My research focuses on large language models (LLMs) and multimodal learning, particularly in healthcare applications. I am interested in building and evaluating AI systems, from curating high-quality datasets to developing models that work reliably in real-world settings. Most recently, I have been exploring how LLMs can enable more realistic and grounded clinical interactions. As part of this effort, I developed PatientSim, a persona-driven framework for simulating realistic doctor-patient conversations grounded in real medical records.
